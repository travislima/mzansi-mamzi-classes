Week 1 • Your emotions

Hi {$name},

Can we talk about something that doesn't get nearly enough airtime? The emotional side of pregnancy.

Everyone asks how you're feeling physically. But nobody really asks about the rest of it. The excitement that suddenly turns into anxiety at 2am. The guilt about not feeling excited enough. The irritability that comes out of nowhere. The crying at adverts.

Here's the thing: all of it is normal.

Your hormones are doing incredible things right now. Progesterone and oestrogen are at levels your body has never experienced before. Your brain is literally rewiring itself to prepare for motherhood. Of course your emotions are all over the place.

What might be showing up for you

•  Mood swings that feel extreme or unpredictable
•  Anxiety about the birth, the baby, or whether you'll be a good mom
•  Feeling overwhelmed by decisions (the nursery, the birth plan, the name)
•  Irritability with your partner, family, or colleagues
•  Unexpected sadness or tearfulness
•  Excitement followed immediately by fear

What actually helps

Name it. "I'm feeling anxious right now" is more powerful than you think. Once you name the feeling, it loses some of its grip.
Lower the bar. You don't need to have everything figured out. You don't need to feel magical every day. Some days you just need to get through the day, and that's enough.
Move your body. Even a 15-minute walk can shift your mood. Gentle movement helps regulate those hormone surges.
Talk about it. Tell your partner, a friend, or your midwife. Saying "I'm struggling a bit" is not weakness. It's wisdom.
Limit the noise. If social media is making you feel worse, step away. If a particular person's advice is stressing you out, it's okay to set a boundary.

When to reach out for more support

If you're feeling persistently low, anxious to the point where it's affecting your daily life, or having thoughts that frighten you — please talk to your midwife or doctor. Antenatal depression and anxiety are real, they're common, and they're treatable. There is no shame in asking for help.
